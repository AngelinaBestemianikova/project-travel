Temple Prambanan

Prambanan Temple is a Hindu temple located in Yogyakarta, the most populous island of Java, Indonesia, one of the most beautiful countries in the world. This Hindu temple, also known as Rara Jonggrang, was built around the 9th century.

This temple is dedicated to the Hindu deity Lord Shiva. It is now the largest Hindu temple in Indonesia and the second largest temple in Southeast Asia, making it a UNESCO World Heritage Site for its art, architecture, and history.

The Prambanan temple complex consists of three zones, the first of which is the outer zone, marked by a rectangular wall. The second is the central region containing hundreds of smaller sanctuaries, and the third is the most sacred inner region containing eight main sanctuaries and eight secondary sanctuaries. The 3 main shrines, called are dedicated to the three Gods of the Trimurti: Brahma, Vishnu, and Shiva Mahadeva.
